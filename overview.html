The Economics of Nature-Based Adaptation (ENBA) app allows you to explore current and future risks from coastal hazards and compare the cost-effectiveness of nature-based adaptation (green) to a coastal armoring strategy (gray) across coastal Ventura County.

Results suggest that nature-based adaptation approaches are as effective in reducing damages as coastal armoring approaches.

The costs for both management scenarios are about the same $1B, with nature-based adaptation slightly lower the coastal armoring.

Decision-makers can explore which alternatives perform the best across a variety of assumptions and results can be explored using different assumptions about: the frequency of storm evens/coastal hazards/SLR; the discount rate used; and, the monetary value of ecosystem services. See pages 64-66, 68 & 69 of the report.

The key steps in this approach which you can explore in the differnt Tabs of the app are:

  • Exposure: to explore different inundation scenarios;
  • Risk: to explore different hazards, discount rates, storm frequencys, and geographies (municipality, city or other unit);
  • Solutions: to compare management scenarios (green/gray), discount rate, value of ecosystem services (i.e. value of wetland) = cost benefit curve of two management scenarios .
